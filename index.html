My Story

My story begins in my hometown in New Jersey, shaped early by two defining forces: math and soccer. The first influence came from my mother: a Moroccan immigrant, a gifted mathematician, and an exceptional teacher. Excellence was never optional; it was the expectation.

Math always came naturally, but the reward of effort was cemented when I placed first in the NJ Math League. By seventh grade, I had mastered the math section of the SAT. Soccer trained my discipline, while math trained my mind. Moving to Texas, I played for FC Dallas while earning a perfect score on AP Calculus BC.

I chose UT Dallas for Actuarial Science to unify mathematical rigor with long-term thinking. After a setback with an ACL injury, I redirected my focus, teaching myself how to study and immersing myself in the actuarial world. I passed Exams P and FM, learning that growth compounds quickly across math, problem-solving, and discipline.

Now in 2026, I am pursuing my Master’s in Business Analytics and AI. I am positioning myself to be versatile: prepared to grow as an actuary or step into new analytical domains where data and decision-making intersect.
