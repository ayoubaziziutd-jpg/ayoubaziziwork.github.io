My Story

My story begins in my hometown in New Jersey, shaped early by two defining forces: math and soccer. My first and most influential mentor was my mother—a Moroccan immigrant, a gifted mathematician, and an exceptional teacher. From day one, she taught me everything she knew. Having her as a guide was a true cheat code: she made math intuitive and exciting, and her immigrant work ethic instilled a standard I learned to live by early on. Excellence was never optional; it was the expectation.

Math always came naturally, but the first time I truly felt the reward of disciplined effort was in sixth grade, when I placed first in my district in the NJ Math League competition. That moment mattered. A teacher pulled me aside and suggested I consider becoming an actuary. A seed was planted. I didn’t yet know how far it would grow, but the direction was set. By seventh grade, I had mastered the math section of the SAT.

At the same time, soccer was my other passion. I had played for as long as I could remember, competing at the highest levels in New Jersey while staying ahead academically. Math and soccer were never competing interests; they reinforced one another. One trained my analytical thinking, the other built discipline, resilience, and focus.

When I moved to Texas my freshman year of high school, the environment changed, but my mentality did not. The competition—both academically and athletically—was stronger. I played for FC Dallas and my high school varsity team while continuing to push academically, earning a perfect score on AP Calculus BC as a junior, scoring highly on AP Statistics as a senior, and performing strongly on the SAT. Even with a full schedule, I made time to give back—tutoring students of all ages in math and SAT preparation and training young athletes in soccer.

I chose the University of Texas at Dallas to pursue Actuarial Science, a major that unified everything I had been building since childhood: mathematical rigor, structured problem-solving, and long-term thinking. The transition to college was not smooth. I faced an identity conflict and academic challenges that demanded structure I had not yet developed.

Then came the turning point: I tore my ACL and meniscus. During the eleven months away from soccer, I slowed down, reassessed, and rebuilt. I taught myself how to study and immersed myself in the actuarial world—joining the Actuarial Club, attending company seminars, and discovering the depth of the profession.

Actuarial science became my intellectual passport. It opened doors to finance, programming, and advanced statistics. I learned that every dataset carries a narrative, and the real challenge is learning how to read it forward. Using the past as evidence, there are infinitely many ways to model and shape the future.

I built my foundation during sophomore and junior year and fully capitalized on it as a senior. I worked on hands-on projects in R, built dashboards in Tableau, and used SQL and Excel to structure data efficiently. Most importantly, I passed Actuarial Exams P and FM, developing discipline, confidence, and momentum.

Since graduating, I have continued building—earning certifications, preparing for my third actuarial exam, and competing in an actuarial case competition using a hybrid Chain-Ladder and Bornhuetter–Ferguson reserving approach.

It is now 2026, and I am pursuing a master’s degree in Business Analytics and AI. This program strengthens my actuarial foundation while expanding my reach into advanced analytics and data science. I am intentionally positioning myself to be versatile—ready to grow as an actuary or pivot into broader analytics roles wherever opportunity and impact align.
